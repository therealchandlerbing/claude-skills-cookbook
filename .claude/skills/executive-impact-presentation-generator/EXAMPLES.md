## Example 1: Product Launch Recommendation

### Context
SaaS company seeking board approval to launch new product line in competitive market.

### Input Conversation

**User:**
```
I need a board presentation to get approval for launching our new analytics product in Q2 2025.
```

**Claude:**
```
I'll help you create a board-ready presentation for your product launch approval.
Let me gather some key information:

1. What's your specific recommendation and ask?
2. Who is your target market and what's the market size?
3. What investment/resources are you requesting?
4. What are your key success metrics and projections?
5. Any brand customization needed?
```

**User:**
```
Recommendation: Launch "DataPulse Analytics" in Q2 2025 with $2M budget

Market:
- Mid-market B2B SaaS companies
- TAM: $50M annually
- 3 main competitors (Tableau, Looker, Power BI)
- We have competitive advantage in real-time data + AI predictions

Investment needed:
- $1.2M product development
- $500K go-to-market
- $300K infrastructure
- 5 new hires (2 engineers, 2 sales, 1 marketing)

Projections:
- 3 pilot customers already committed
- $8M revenue Year 1
- $25M revenue Year 3
- 18-month payback period

Risks:
- Technical complexity (AI model training)
- Competitive response
- Market timing

Timeline:
- Development: Jan-Mar 2025
- Beta: April 2025
- GA Launch: June 2025

Brand: Use our company colors - Primary #0B5FD0, Accent #6D4ACD
```

### Output: PowerPoint Outline (Sample)

```markdown
# DataPulse Analytics Launch Proposal
**Board of Directors Meeting**
**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Presenter**: Chief Product Officer
**Date**: December 2024
**Objective**: Secure $2M budget approval for Q2 2025 product launch

---

## Slide 1: Title Slide

**Visual**:
- Company logo (top left)
- Title: "DataPulse Analytics Launch Proposal"
- Subtitle: "Capturing $50M Market Opportunity"
- Presenter: Chief Product Officer
- Date: December 2024
- Background: Subtle gradient (brand blue #0B5FD0)

**Script**:
"Good morning, Board. Thank you for your time. Today I'm presenting our recommendation to launch DataPulse Analytics, our new real-time analytics platform. This is a 20-minute presentation covering market opportunity, product strategy, financial projections, and our launch plan. My ask today is approval for a $2M investment to launch in Q2 2025. Let's begin."

---

## Slide 2: Executive Summary

**Visual**:
- Large headline: "Recommendation: Launch DataPulse Analytics in Q2 2025"
- Three key metrics displayed as large numbers:
  - "$50M TAM"
  - "$8M Y1 Revenue"
  - "18-Month Payback"
- Timeline graphic: "Launch: June 2025"
- Investment ask: "$2M Total Investment"

**Script**:
"Here's the bottom line: I'm recommending we launch DataPulse Analytics in Q2 2025 with a $2M investment. We've identified a $50 million total addressable market where we have a clear competitive advantage. Our projections show $8 million in Year 1 revenue with an 18-month payback period. We have 3 pilot customers already committed. We need board approval today to hit our June 2025 launch date. Let me walk you through why this is the right move."

**Speaking Notes**:
- Emphasize "$8M Y1 Revenue" - this is the hook
- Pause for 3 seconds after stating recommendation
- Watch for nodding/questions
- Transition: "Let me provide context on the market opportunity..."

**Q&A Anticipation**:
- Q: "Why now? Why not wait?"
  A: "We have market timing advantage. Our 3 pilot customers are ready now, and our competitor Tableau just raised prices 30%, creating a window of opportunity. Waiting 6 months risks losing this advantage."

- Q: "What if we can't hit $8M Y1?"
  A: "Our base case is actually $6M. The $8M assumes moderate market response. Even at $6M, we hit payback in 22 months, which exceeds our hurdle rate."

## Slide 3: Market Opportunity

**Visual**:
- Title: "Large, Growing Market with Clear Gap"
- TAM breakdown (pie chart):
  - Total market: $50M annually
  - Current competitors: Tableau (40%), Looker (25%), Power BI (20%)
  - Unserved/underserved: 15% ($7.5M)
- Growth arrow: "12% CAGR through 2028"
- Gap callout: "No real-time + AI solution exists"

**Script**:
"The market opportunity is substantial. We're targeting the mid-market B2B SaaS analytics space, which is $50 million annually and growing at 12% per year. As you can see, three players dominate: Tableau, Looker, and Power BI. But here's the critical insight: none of them offer true real-time data combined with AI-powered predictions. That's our white space. And it represents $7.5 million in immediate opportunity, with 15% of the market actively looking for a solution like ours."

**Speaking Notes**:
- Point to the gap in the market when saying "white space"
- Emphasize "real-time + AI" as our differentiator
- Be ready to explain "mid-market" definition if asked

**Visual**:
- Title: "Why We'll Win: Real-Time + AI Predictions"

| Feature | Tableau | Looker | Power BI | **DataPulse** |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|---------------|
| Real-time data | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

| Sub-second queries | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Mid-market pricing | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |

- Customer quote: "We've been waiting for a solution like this" - Pilot Customer

**Script**:
"Why will we win? Two reasons: real-time data processing and AI-powered predictions. Let me show you how we stack up against the competition. Tableau, Looker, and Power BI all have 5-15 minute data refresh cycles. We're sub-second. None of them offer predictive analytics powered by AI. We do. And we're priced for mid-market companies, not enterprises. This combination doesn't exist in the market today. That's why we already have 3 pilot customers committed, and this is before we've even officially launched."

## Slide 5: Financial Projections

  - Y3: $25M

  - Go-to-Market: $500K (25%)
```

## Customization Examples

### Adjust for Audience

**For Board** (strategic, high-level):
- Fewer slides (8-12)
- More whitespace
- Focus on outcomes and financials
- Minimal technical detail

**For Executive Team** (tactical, cross-functional):
- More slides (12-18)
- Departmental impacts
- Resource implications
- Execution details

**For Team** (operational, detailed):
- Most slides (15-25)
- Step-by-step plans
- Role clarity
- Timelines and dependencies

### Adjust for Time

**5-minute pitch**:
- 3-5 slides
- Recommendation, Why, Ask
- One chart maximum

**15-minute presentation**:
- 8-10 slides
- Standard structure
- Key charts and data

**30-minute deep dive**:
- 15-20 slides
- Comprehensive analysis
- Multiple visualizations
- Detailed appendix

## Success Patterns

### What Makes These Examples Work

1. **Recommendation First**: Never bury the lead
2. **Data-Driven**: Every claim backed by numbers
3. **Visual Hierarchy**: Most important info = largest/first
4. **Clear Ask**: Audience knows exactly what you want
5. **Risk Acknowledgment**: Address concerns proactively
6. **Action-Oriented**: Always end with next steps

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ Too many slides (> 25 for a 30-min presentation)
❌ Text-heavy slides (> 6 bullets)
❌ Burying the recommendation (put it slide 2)
❌ No clear ask (what decision do you need?)
❌ Ignoring risks (address them head-on)
❌ No next steps (always end with actions)
